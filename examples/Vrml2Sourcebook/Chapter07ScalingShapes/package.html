Rotations can transform the orientation of a shape in any direction.

Scale transformations grow or shrink a local coordinate system by a scaling factor in the X, Y, and Z directions. Transform operations are bottom-up: children shapes are first scaled, rotated, then translated. Order is fixed, independent of the field order that happens to occur in the Transform node.
